### Bike 1 had a charging problem. Diagnosing it meant pulling the left side cover and testing the stator output directly, which pointed clearly at the stator itself rather than the regulator or wiring. I sourced a replacement, installed it, and had the bike running. That part went smoothly.

### Then I made a mistake. With the left side already open and the bike on the stand, I decided to tear into the clutch -- partly out of curiosity, partly because it seemed like a logical thing to check while I was in there. The clutch was fine. Everything looked exactly as it should, the teardown accomplished nothing, and reassembling it cost an afternoon I didn't need to spend. It was an early lesson in the discipline of not pulling things apart without a reason. Bike 1 sold shortly after, and the proceeds went straight toward Bike 2.
