### Between my first and third years of school I ran a one-man exterior painting and staining business out of Airdrie called Innovative Exterior. The logistics were simple: a 2002 Duramax with 430,000+ kilometres on it, a trailer, and whatever equipment the job called for. I had no formal business training and no painting background going in. What I did have was a willingness to figure things out, a truck that refused to quit, and enough summers ahead of me to make the mistakes worth making.

### Over two seasons I picked up about a dozen clients, mostly fences, decks, pergolas, and house exteriors across Airdrie. None of it was glamorous work, and the hourly rate I was effectively paying myself probably wouldn't pass scrutiny, but every job got done and every client was happy with the result. The business wound down naturally after my second summer when my co-op at TransCanada Turbines made the economics pretty clear. No regrets, it taught me more about running a job than any class did.

#### Renting a boom lift to reach the upper sections of a two-storey is the kind of problem-solving the business ran on. If the job needed it, you figured out how to get it.
