### The Car

This was my first real enthusiast car, and the one I'd argue taught me the most. I bought it in the spring I graduated high school for $5,000 and over the course of roughly five years of ownership, probably put $20,000 into it. Worth every cent.

It was a JDM SW20, which in 1995 meant the third-generation 3S-GTE: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four producing around 245 hp in factory trim. The JDM '95 also got revised swaybars, an upgraded fuel pump, updated taillights, and a newer turbo over earlier revisions, all of which made it a desirable base. When I bought it, it had relatively low kilometres for its age (around 150k), had been repainted at some point, and came with a few mild bolt-ons: aftermarket wheels, a muffler delete, a "cold air intake," a Greddy blow-off valve, and a Blitz turbo timer. It looked like exactly what it was: a good foundation.

### Performance Modifications

The first mods I added were on the power side, and relatively inexpensive for what they delivered. I installed a manual boost controller and an Innovate wideband O2 sensor paired with a boost gauge, which let me dial boost up to around 15 psi safely. The car was never dyno'd, but it was genuinely fast for a '90s RWD mid-engine car at that power level.

The bigger investment came with the equal-length turbo manifold, a Greddy intercooler upgrade, and custom charge pipes fabbed up by a local shop to make everything fit. Replacing the stock log-style manifold with an equal-length unit meaningfully improves spool characteristics and exhaust flow, and combined with the front-mount intercooler and custom piping the setup was a proper step up from stock.

### Suspension and Wheels

On the handling side, I fitted Tein coilovers and Megan Racing adjustable control arms. The coilovers brought the ride height down meaningfully and added proper adjustability, while the control arms were necessary to correct camber geometry once the car was sitting lower. The combination made an already sharp-handling car genuinely rewarding to push.

For wheels, I went with Zedd 18-inch wheels in a staggered fitment: 18x9.5 up front with 225/40R18 Michelin Pilot Sport 4s, and 18x10.5 in the rear with 255/40R18s. To make the wider rear wheels fit properly, I added fender flares all around, which filled the arches and gave the car an aggressive stance that suited it well.

### The Paint

Toward the end of my ownership I decided to repaint the car. The previous owner had already repainted it once, and after years of modifications and some body prep work it felt like the right time to do it properly. A buddy of mine is a painter by trade, so we prepped the car together in his garage over several weeks and then trailered it to his shop on a weekend and banged out the paint job.

The colour is a BMW code: a deep brown with a warm copper and red metallic flake that shifts dramatically in different light. In direct sun it reads almost burgundy. In shade it looks nearly black. It was a significant departure from the non-stock red the car wore before, and it completely changed the character of the car.

<br>

I eventually traded the car for a truck and some cash at the end of my ownership to cover my first year of university. It was the right call financially, but it was a hard one to make. Between the $5k purchase and everything that went into it, it was the car that shaped how I think about building cars: do it methodically, do it right, and document it. I still think about the methanol kit sitting in a box.
