### The Diagnosis

I picked up a dead 2019 Sony 55" for free and started diagnosing it. A quick search confirmed this model had a well-documented failure mode: a chip on the main board, located between the HDMI ports and the AV inputs, had a known tendency to fail. Before assuming the worst, I worked through the simpler possibilities first: measuring supply voltages at the T-con board and checking the main board for anything more obvious. The voltages looked correct. The failure pointed squarely at the chip.

Sourcing a replacement wasn't the problem. Finding one pre-flashed with the firmware for this specific TV model was. Without the right firmware the chip is useless, and I don't have the hardware to flash a microcontroller from scratch. The TV went to recycling. Not every repair ends with the thing working, and sometimes the limiting factor isn't the repair itself but the parts ecosystem around it.
