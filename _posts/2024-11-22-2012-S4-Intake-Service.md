### After finding coolant residue in the two rear intake ports, the car needed a proper teardown. My working theory at this point was a failed PCV, and on these engines the PCV housing is integrated into a coolant pipe where a crack at that interface could mix air and coolant in a way that presented exactly like what I had found. The plan was to pull the intake runners, clean up the contamination, and get a better look.

#### Off the car: the supercharger, both intake runners, the throttle body, and the fuel rails. A solid morning of removal before anything useful can happen.

![Intake components off the car and laid out on the workbench](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/1.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### With the runners off, both banks of intake ports are fully exposed. Most of the ports were actually surprisingly clean, the coolant intrusion had done a reasonable job of steam cleaning the valves on the way through. The two rear ports were the exception.

![Engine with intake runners removed, both cylinder banks exposed](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/2.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### The residue left in the rear ports from the coolant contamination. These needed to be cleared out before anything went back together.

![Coolant residue in a rear intake port](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/3.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

![Rear ports before and after cleaning](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/8-9.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### New upper and lower intake runner gaskets along with a collection of other seals and O-rings needed for this depth of teardown. Having all the hardware ready before starting reassembly saves a lot of grief.

![New gaskets and seals laid out on the bench](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/12.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### Runners going back on with fresh gaskets. The green O-rings on the port faces are the lower intake runner seals.

![Lower intake runners installed and reinstalled with new seals](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/11-13.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### Supercharger back in place. Most of the complexity from here is in the hose and electrical reconnections, of which the 3.0 TFSI has plenty.

![Supercharger being reinstalled](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/14.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### Two days, clean ports, fresh seals, and a running car. The coolant source still wasn't fully diagnosed, but the intake was in significantly better shape than when I started.

![Connor giving a thumbs up at completion of the two-day service](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-22/15.jpg){: width="100%" }
