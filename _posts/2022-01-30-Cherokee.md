### The Find

I found this 2008 Jeep Grand Cherokee in the classifieds knowing it didn't run. Drove three hours to Sparwood, BC with the Duramax and a U-Haul trailer to pick it up. What I hadn't fully thought through was loading a non-running vehicle onto a trailer on snow-covered ground. A second truck, some tow ropes, a snatch block, and a fair bit of improvised rigging sorted it out, and I was back on the road without too much drama.

Back home, I confirmed the engine was seized by trying to turn it over at the crank pulley. The teardown pointed first at the accessory drive and valvetrain, but pulling the cylinder head gave the real answer: cylinder 8 had a hole in the piston. The likely cause was a clogged injector running that cylinder lean and hot enough to burn through. While boring the cylinder and replacing the piston was technically possible, the risk of metal debris circulating through the oiling system after an event like that made a full engine swap the only sensible path.

I sourced a low-mileage 5.7L HEMI out of a 2009 Dodge Ram, which is a largely compatible swap into the Grand Cherokee platform. The job took about a month working through it in the evenings and weekends. The Cherokee went back together, ran well, and sold a few weeks later for a solid return on the investment.
