### I bought this car knowing it had an oil leak. On the B8 S4, the oil filter housing gasket is a known weak point on the 3.0 TFSI and matched the symptoms, so it was the first place I looked. What I didn't expect was what I'd find once I started pulling things apart.

#### Getting to the oil filter housing requires removing a bank of vacuum solenoids off the back of the supercharger first. Tedious, but manageable once you know they're there.

![Vacuum solenoids on the back of the supercharger](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-17/20241117_112718.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### Sure enough, the housing gasket had been seeping. Minor, but confirmed.

![Oil filter housing area showing oil residue](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-17/20241117_112639.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### While I was in there I noticed pink coolant pooling in the valley between the cylinder banks, which I wasn't looking for. A failed PCV is a well-documented issue on these engines and a common cause of exactly this. With coolant showing up somewhere it shouldn't, the supercharger came off for a closer look. A friend lent a hand with the removal.

![Friend assisting with supercharger removal](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-17/20241117_145915.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

#### This is what the concern was about. The two rear cylinder intake ports had coolant residue coating the port walls and tumble flaps. Coolant doesn't belong here. At this point I knew there was a contamination path into the intake tract, but the exact source wasn't confirmed yet.

![Coolant residue in rear intake port](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-17/20241117_145928.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

![Coolant residue in second rear intake port](/assets/img/Mechanical Experience/2012 Audi S4/2024-11-17/20241117_145932.jpg){: width="100%" }

<br>

### The oil leak was the easy part. The coolant in the intake was the real find, and it pointed to something that would need a proper teardown to address. That became the next session.
