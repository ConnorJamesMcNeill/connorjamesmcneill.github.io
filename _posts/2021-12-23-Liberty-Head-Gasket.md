### The Diagnosis

I picked up this 2007 Jeep Liberty cheap, knowing it wasn't running. The seller said it was overheating, which could mean almost anything. A compression check narrowed it down quickly: cylinder 6 read zero while everything else was normal. That's a head gasket until proven otherwise.

The 3.7L PowerTech V6 is not a particularly difficult engine to work on, but getting to the heads requires pulling the cam chains first, so the disassembly goes deeper than it might look from the outside. The timing cover comes off, the chains come out, and then the heads are free. Both went to the machine shop for inspection and resurfacing. When they came back, I found the rear two valve seats had been loose, which is a known weakness on these aluminum heads. The shop pressed new ones in as part of the job. All in, the machine work ran about $1,100.

Reassembly went back together with new Fel-Pro head gaskets, cam chains reinstalled, and timing set. It started on the first crank and hasn't overheated since.
