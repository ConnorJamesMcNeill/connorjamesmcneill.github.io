### The project brief for ENME 493 was to take an existing product, apply the analysis methods from the course, and improve it in a meaningful way. Our team selected the Mammut Smart 2.0, a geometrically assisted belay device, as the baseline. The Smart 2.0 is a well-regarded piece of gear, but its assisted-braking geometry comes at the cost of versatility. It cannot be configured as an ATC guide mode device, which limits its utility on multi-pitch and wall climbing where guide mode is standard practice. The goal was to expand the design to support both modes while maintaining a competitive strength-to-weight ratio.

### The design was approached with low-volume manufacturing in mind from the start. The main body is 7075 aluminum, laser cut and hard anodized. The rope guides are CNC machined 316 stainless steel, chosen for its corrosion resistance and durability at the rope contact surfaces. The handle grip is ABS, either injection molded or 3D printed depending on volume. Off-the-shelf spacers and rivets handle the fastener set entirely, keeping the custom part count low and the bill of materials scalable.

#### Exploded view of the redesigned assembly. The 7075 aluminum main body carries both rope slots, with the 316 stainless rope guides, ABS handle grip, and off-the-shelf spacers and rivets completing the assembly. The second rope slot enabling guide mode is integrated directly into the main body profile.
