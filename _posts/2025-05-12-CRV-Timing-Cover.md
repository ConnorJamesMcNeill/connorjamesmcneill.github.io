### The Job

The 2017 CRV runs Honda's 1.5L L15B7 turbocharged inline-four, making 190 hp and 179 lb-ft. My uncle regularly has me do work on his vehicles and passes my name along to friends; his family runs mostly Hondas, and word travels quickly when the bill comes back a fraction of what the dealer quoted. This one belonged to a friend of his. Honda had suggested a valve cover and timing cover re-seal and quoted $1,100 CAD for the privilege. He did it for $500.

Both covers were seeping, not a structural failure, just aged gasket material doing what it does over time. The timing chain itself had recently been replaced, so that was not part of this job. The task was to pull both covers, clean the mating surfaces, and reseal them. The valve cover is a black plastic unit with a press-in gasket. The timing cover is cast aluminum and runs a formed gasket along its perimeter plus a front main seal at the crankshaft. Getting the timing cover off requires working around the front of the engine from both above and below, clearing enough of the accessory drive to get the cover free.
