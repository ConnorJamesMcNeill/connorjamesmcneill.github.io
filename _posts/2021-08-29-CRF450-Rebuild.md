### With the failure mode understood, the rebuild parts list came together: new crankcase halves, new piston and rod, new oil pump shaft and drive gear, a Hinson clutch basket, and a full set of seals and gaskets. Every wear item got replaced while it was apart. There was no point in reusing anything in the bottom end after an event like this, and with the cases already split it cost very little extra to do it right.

### The assembly went through the full sequence -- bottom end into the new cases, cases joined and sealed, top end installed, then back into the frame. By the end of the summer the bike was running. The final accounting came out about a thousand dollars in the red across the whole project, which was the expected outcome going in. The knowledge of how a high-revving single-cylinder four-stroke comes apart and goes back together was worth considerably more than that, and it turned out to be directly relevant the following year when I joined the FSAE team.
