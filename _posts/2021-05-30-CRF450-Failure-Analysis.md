### With Bike 1 sold, attention shifted fully to the failure bike. The crankcase had a hole punched through it near the main bearing -- a catastrophic failure mode that takes some working through to understand. A connecting rod doesn't exit a case on its own; something has to let the bearing go first. The most consistent explanation across the evidence was oil starvation leading to main bearing failure, which then allowed the rod to drive through the case wall. Whether that was a maintenance issue, a pre-existing oiling problem, or something that happened in a specific incident wasn't possible to determine from the hardware alone.

### The teardown confirmed the extent of the damage and ruled out any path that didn't involve a new case. Boring the cylinder and swapping the piston on the original cases would have been possible if the cases were sound, but with debris circulating through the bottom end after an event like this, a full case replacement was the only sensible call. Parts were sourced and the rebuild would follow.

#### The hole in the crankcase. The exit point is near the main bearing journal -- consistent with bearing failure followed by connecting rod contact.
