### Separate from the drivetrain conversion, the client also wanted to add power steering to the 356. The original car had a manual rack and no provisions for an electric assist unit. The solution was to source an electric power steering rack from a Volkswagen MK5 Golf/Jetta, which is a compact, self-contained unit with a known geometry, and design a custom mount that would locate it correctly in the 356 front suspension while tying into the existing chassis structure.

### The mount went through several variations, each exploring different chassis fixture methods and manufacturing approaches. Modifying the original 356 chassis was not an option, so every iteration had to work with existing structure. The final VAR5 uses u-bolts to clamp to a section of the chassis without any permanent modification, combined with a plate and tube construction that is consistent with the fabrication approach used throughout the rest of the project.

#### VAR3, showing both sides with an intermediate cross-bar layout. This version explored a simpler tube-only construction before the combined plate and tube approach of the final design was settled on.
